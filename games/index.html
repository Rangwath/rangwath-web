Here you'll discover a collection of small games created solely by me using the Unity or Godot game engines and utilizing a mix of free and paid assets. These games are usually inspired by courses on GameDev.tv or Udemy.

Take a moment to explore my humble creations. I hope you enjoy exploring these games as much as I enjoyed creating them.

Purple Quest (2024)

Introducing my debut creation with Godot 4. For the first time, I ventured into crafting my own pixel art, bringing to life a simple yet charming 2D platformer game.

Compare it to my very first game, I've focused on polishing the controls to offer a much more pleasant playthrough experience and also much more challenging game design. Additionally, there is a fully functional highscore system, making it more entertaining as players strive to beat their previous records.

Little Deathmatch (2022)

Introducing my pioneering venture into the realm of 3D multiplayer game development powered by the Photon Engine. Inspired by the Online Multiplayer Game In Unity Course from Udemy, I present this simple 3D multiplayer FPS game.

This project represents my initial foray into the world of 3D and multiplayer game development, incorporating great low-poly assets from the Synty Store. It reflects my commitment to honing my skills and exploring the exciting possibilities of interactive gaming.

Cave Escape (2020)

Introducing my debut creation, a simple and short 2D platformer that marks my initiation into the world of game development. Developed following the Complete C# Unity Game Developer 2D Course by GameDev.tv, this game is a reflection to my early strides in the Unity game development realm.

As I look back on this first endeavor, I recognize so many areas that could be improved, but this project stands as a nice reminder of my game dev beginnings.
